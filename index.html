- Infrastructure On the Street -

Chorus Street Markings

The ground is marked so that contractors know where cables run or need to be run.

Chorus Underground Cable Access

A cover placed over the cables run underground so that contractors can access the cables if needed.

Chorus Small junction Box

A small circular junction box for taking in, splitting off and maintaining connections.

Chorus Big Junction Box

A large rectangular junction box, it takes all the connections and splits them off to nearby homes and businesses. It also makes maintainance on the cables easier.

- Infrastructure At Home -

Fiber Cable

The fiber optics cable connecting from the street a house. This carries the internet connection from the ISP.

Chorus Fiber Box

The Chorus fiber box takes in the fiber connection from the street and processes it so it can be used throughout the house. It outputs through an ethernet connection to a router.

Router

The router takes the connection from the fiber box and transmits it either wirelessly, over WiFi, or wired through an ethernet cable. Devices can connect to the router to access the internet.

Devices

Now the user can connect and use the internet on all their devices such as smart phones, computers and game consoles.

- Transmission and Connected Devices -

Spark WiFi Zone

Around the city Spark makes use of their telephone boxes to distribute WiFi for their customers.

Bus Stop Time Board

Metlink bus time boards have a little antenna on top so they can receive up to date information on the current bus locations and times.

CCTV Camera

A CCTV surveillance camera will connect wired or wirelessly to a network to transmit a live video feed.

Digital Billboard

This digital billboard cycles through images. It would be connected to a network so that the images can be changed and swapped as new customers want advertisments.
